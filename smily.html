B.R. Ambedka

Bhimrao Ranji Ambedkar ( better known as Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar ) (14 April 1891 â€“ 6 December 1956) was a prominent Indian lawyer, economist, political leader and social reformer. He worked hard for untouchability and abolition of caste . He was the first Union Law Minister of independent India and the architect of the Constitution . He earned a Ph.D from Columbia University and a D.Sc (Doctorate) from the University of London, a rare honor. He did research in law , social and economic sciences .
Initially, he worked as a lawyer, teacher and economist. Later he worked for India's independence, publication of newspapers, socio-political rights of Dalits and constitution of India. In 1956, Dalits converted en masse to Buddhism when he embraced Buddhism. In 1990, the Government of India awarded him the Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award, posthumously. A memorable leader in the history of India. His birthday is celebrated as "Ambedkar Jayanti" for his remarkable contribution.
